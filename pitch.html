01 · The problem

Renters can't verify who they're about to pay.

Before paying, a renter in urban Kenya has no way to check whether a listing, an agent, or a landlord is genuine. That forces a choice: walk from estate to estate looking for physical "to let" signs, or trust an unverified listing and risk a deposit on someone with no real relationship to the property.

No.1

No accountable identity

Anyone can post a photo and a phone number, with no check they're who they claim or have any legal relationship to the property.

No.2

No visibility into true cost

Deposit, agent fees, water, garbage and service charges surface piecemeal — sometimes only after a "viewing fee" is already paid.

No.3

No memory of bad actors

A landlord or agent who scams one tenant faces no consequence, and can relist under a new number the following week.

02 · The solution

A Trust Ledger, not an invented score.

Every listing carries a timestamped, specific record of real actions taken to confirm it's genuine: landlord identity confirmed against national ID, the unit visited in person, the full cost disclosed upfront, and reviews from tenants who actually lived there. A score like "91% trusted" can't be checked — nobody knows how it was calculated. Every line in a ledger is a specific, falsifiable claim instead.

A ledger can be checked. A score can only be believed.

Today, honestly: verification is manual. Our team confirms identity by phone against a national ID, visits units in person, and records true cost directly from landlord and tenant. This doesn't scale infinitely yet — it's the only version of verification that's fully true from day one, designed to scale in stages as volume grows.

06 · Competitive landscape

Everyone competes on inventory. Nobody competes on trust.

No current Kenyan platform combines a searchable listing with a checkable verification record and an itemised true cost shown before commitment. That's the open space.

Existing listing apps

Forty-plus, and counting

  • Compete on inventory volume and search filters
  • No verifiable identity attached to any listing
  • No checkable history behind a landlord or agent
  • Revenue: advertising and agent subscriptions
NyumbaIQ

The trust layer, not another listings app

  • Competes on whether the listing can be trusted
  • Every listing carries a checkable Trust Ledger
  • Itemised true cost disclosed before commitment
  • Revenue: per-search Hunt Pass, not ad-driven
Adjacent, not competing

Rent collection & property management tools

  • Operate after a landlord already has a tenant
  • NyumbaIQ operates before that relationship exists
  • Seen as future integration partners, not rivals
  • We stay in trust and verification only
07 · Who we serve, in order

Renters first. Then everyone who needs them to trust the system.

Each stage only works once the one before it is already working. First, house hunters — renters and buyers, the most frequent, most painful version of the trust gap. Second, individual landlords — the same Trust Ledger sold back to them, since a verified landlord attracts serious tenants faster. Third, managing agencies — verified leads once real search volume exists. We don't build rent collection, mortgage tools, an off-plan marketplace, investor dashboards, or social features yet. None of those strengthen the Trust Ledger today, so they wait.

08 · Go-to-market

Four phases. Each one a precondition for the next.

Not run in parallel. Each phase only starts once the one before it is already working.

01

Manual verification, one Nairobi neighbourhood

Verify listings by hand in a single walkable cluster: phone confirmation of identity, in-person unit visits, direct cost confirmation.

02

Renter acquisition through the pain point

Reach renters where they already search — Facebook and WhatsApp groups — leading with the fraud story, not a feature list.

03

Landlord onboarding, once demand is proven

Approach individual landlords with real usage data showing tenant interest, not a cold pitch to a hypothetical audience.

04

Agency partnerships

Once volume is meaningful, offer managing agencies qualified leads — a source of serious tenants, not a threat to their tools.

10 · Traction and milestones

Early stage, stated honestly.

This is a validated concept, not a live product with users — and we'd rather show you exactly where it stands than round up.

What exists today

A complete, functioning showcase explaining the problem, the mechanism, and the pricing model
A documented Trust Ledger concept with a full working example
Real primary research already completed: 25 survey respondents, 30+ interviews
A defined, sequenced go-to-market plan starting in a single Nairobi neighbourhood

What doesn't exist yet

Zero real verified listings are live
No paying customer has completed a transaction yet
Landlord onboarding has not started

Near-term milestones, in order: complete manual verification of an initial set of real listings in one Nairobi neighbourhood; convert the first 100 paying Hunt Pass users from that same neighbourhood; use that real usage data to begin landlord conversations. Each is a precondition for the next, not a parallel workstream.

11 · The ask
